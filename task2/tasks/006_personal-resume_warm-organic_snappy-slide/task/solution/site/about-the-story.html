Chapter 01 — Origins

Grew up making
things pretty loud.

I was fourteen when I printed my first zine on a busted photocopier in my dad's garage in Tucson. Forty-eight pages of collaged photographs, hand-lettered rants about strip-mall architecture, and ink-smudged portraits of friends who thought I was wasting my time.

They were right about one thing — I was never going to fit into a cubicle. By eighteen I'd dropped out of a business program, enrolled in a scrappy community college design course, and started freelancing for local bands who couldn't afford anyone better. That was the beginning.

Chapter 02 — Philosophy

Design should provoke, not decorate.

I believe every brand has a raw nerve — something honest and uncomfortable and true. My job is to find it, amplify it, and turn it into visual language that people can't scroll past. I don't polish things until they're unrecognizable. I sharpen them until they cut.

That means saying no to safe gradients, stock photography, and "can we make the logo bigger." It means championing rough textures, bold geometry, and typography that has something to say beyond the words it spells.

Chapter 03 — The Path

Fifteen years of breaking
and building.

2009 — 2012

The Garage Years

Freelance · Tucson, AZ

Designed flyers, album covers, and merch for local punk bands and DIY venues. Learned that constraints breed creativity — and that a $200 budget can produce work that outshines a $20,000 campaign if you care enough.

2012 — 2015

Junior Designer → Art Director

Cinder & Smoke Agency · Portland, OR

Joined a twelve-person boutique agency and climbed from production work to leading brand identity projects. Spearheaded the rebrand of Timberline Brewing that won a regional AIGA award and tripled their taproom foot traffic.

2015 — 2019

Senior Creative Lead

Foundry Collective · Brooklyn, NY

Directed campaigns for heritage fashion labels, independent publishers, and a James Beard–nominated restaurant group. Built a team of five designers who shared a belief that commercial work could still be art.

2019 — Present

Founder & Creative Director

RAZE Studio · Remote / Everywhere

Launched my own practice to work directly with founders, artists, and cultural institutions who want branding that refuses to blend in. Clients across four continents. Zero stock photos used. Ever.

Chapter 04 — Beliefs

What I stand for.

01

Rough Edges Are Honest

Perfection is a lie brands tell themselves. I leave the grain in, the ink bleed visible, the hand of the maker present. Audiences trust what feels real.

02

Strategy Before Style

A gorgeous identity built on a weak foundation is wallpaper. Every visual decision I make is rooted in positioning, audience insight, and a clear point of view.

03

Say No More Often

The best work comes from discipline — fewer colors, fewer typefaces, fewer compromises. I'd rather do one thing that burns into memory than ten things that fade.

04

Print Isn't Dead

Screens are temporary. A well-printed piece — letterpress, risograph, screen-printed — creates a physical relationship between brand and human that pixels never will.

05

Collaboration Over Ego

I bring strong opinions and hold them loosely. The best outcomes happen when client knowledge and creative instinct collide without anyone pulling rank.

06

Culture Is the Brief

I study music, architecture, food, subcultures, and street art — not just design annuals. The most resonant work borrows from life, not from Dribbble.

Off the Clock

When I'm not designing.

I collect vintage matchbook covers — over 800 at last count — because they're the purest form of micro-branding ever invented. A two-inch canvas, one chance to make someone remember your bar, your motel, your dry cleaner.

I cook obsessively (Oaxacan moles are my current fixation), ride a 1978 Honda CB550 that's held together by optimism, and volunteer at a community print shop teaching teenagers how to set metal type. I believe the best creative directors have lives outside of design that make their design better.
