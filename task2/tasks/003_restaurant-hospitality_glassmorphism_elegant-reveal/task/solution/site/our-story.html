Our Story

A neighborhood bistro born from a shared love of honest food, good wine, and the people who gather around the table.

How It Began

Two Friends, One Dream

Ember & Vine started in 2016 when childhood friends Elena Marchetti and James Calloway decided their city deserved something different — a place where seasonal ingredients met unpretentious warmth, where a Tuesday dinner felt as special as a Saturday celebration.

Elena, a classically trained chef who spent a decade in kitchens from Lyon to Portland, wanted to cook the food she actually loved eating: simple, bold, rooted in what the land offered each week. James, a sommelier with an instinct for pairing and a deep network of small-batch winemakers, envisioned a wine program that told stories with every glass.

Our Philosophy

Rooted in the Season,
Shaped by the Community

We believe the best meals begin long before they reach the plate. They start in the soil of local farms, in the careful hands of growers and foragers, in the rhythm of the seasons that dictate what belongs on your table tonight. Our menu changes not because it's trendy, but because it's honest.

🔥

Fire & Craft

Every dish is cooked with intention over live flame and seasoned cast iron. We char, smoke, and braise to coax depth from the simplest ingredients — a heritage carrot, a dry-aged cut, a handful of wild herbs.

🍇

Vine & Vessel

Our wine list favors small producers who farm sustainably and vinify with minimal intervention. From a crisp Albariño to a brooding Barolo, each bottle is chosen because it has something genuine to say.

🌿

Soil & Source

We partner with twelve local farms within a sixty-mile radius. Deliveries arrive at our back door three mornings a week — greens still cool from the field, eggs still warm from the coop.

Milestones

The Journey So Far

16 Spring

Doors Open

Ember & Vine opens on a quiet corner of Elm Street with 38 seats, a hand-written menu, and a wine list scribbled on brown paper. The first night sells out by word of mouth alone.

17 Winter

The Garden Kitchen

We break ground on our kitchen garden behind the restaurant — raised beds of herbs, edible flowers, and heirloom tomatoes that now supply garnishes and seasonal specials year-round.

19 Autumn

The Vine Room

We expand into the adjoining space, adding The Vine Room — a private dining area for up to 24 guests, complete with a curated cellar wall and a custom harvest table milled from reclaimed oak.

21 Summer

Community Table Launches

Our monthly Community Table dinner series begins — a single long table, a set seasonal menu, and strangers who leave as friends. It becomes our most beloved tradition.

24 Now

Still Growing

Eight years in, we remain the same 38-seat bistro at heart — just with a bigger garden, a deeper cellar, and a community that feels like family. The best chapters are still being written.

The People

Meet the Team

Behind every plate and every pour, there are people who care deeply about what they do.

Elena Marchetti

Chef & Co-Founder

Elena's cooking is instinctive and generous — informed by years in French and Pacific Northwest kitchens, but always guided by what tastes right today. She writes every menu by hand, in pencil, because things change.

James Calloway

Sommelier & Co-Founder

James has a rare gift for making wine approachable without dumbing it down. He'll pour you a glass of something you've never heard of, tell you the story of the family who made it, and change the way you think about Tuesday nights.

Sofia Reyes

Pastry Chef

Sofia joined in 2018 and transformed our dessert program. Her brown-butter financiers and seasonal fruit galettes have become signature endings to every meal — simple, warm, and impossible to forget.

What We Believe

Our Guiding Principles

Seasonality Over Convenience

If it's not in season, it's not on the menu. We'd rather serve three perfect dishes than thirty mediocre ones. The constraints of the calendar are what make our cooking creative.

Warmth Without Pretension

Fine ingredients don't require fine airs. We want you in jeans or a jacket, on a first date or a thousandth dinner. Everyone gets the same generous welcome.

Sustainability as Practice

We compost all kitchen waste, source within sixty miles wherever possible, use whole-animal butchery to minimize waste, and power our kitchen with renewable energy.

Community First

Ten percent of every Community Table dinner goes to the local food bank. We host quarterly fundraiser nights and offer our space to neighborhood organizations at no charge.

Come Be Part of the Story

Whether it's your first visit or your hundredth, there's always a seat at our table. We'd love to welcome you.
