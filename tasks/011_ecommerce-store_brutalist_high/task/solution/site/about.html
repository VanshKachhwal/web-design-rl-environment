Our Story

Built on Craft, Rooted in Community

We started with a simple belief: that everyday goods should be made with intention, sourced with integrity, and built to last. What began as a small workshop in 2016 has grown into a collective of makers, growers, and artisans dedicated to honest craft.

Shop Our Goods
What We Believe

Principles Before Profit

Every decision we make — from sourcing raw materials to choosing our partners — is guided by a commitment to transparency, sustainability, and fair labor. We don't cut corners, and we don't chase trends.

We work directly with small-batch producers, family farms, and independent workshops. No middlemen, no mass production. Each item in our catalog has a name and a story behind it — a ceramicist in the hill country, a weaver in the valley, a woodworker who learned from her grandfather.

Our supply chain is short by design. We believe that knowing where your goods come from isn't a luxury — it's a right. That's why we publish our sourcing details and visit every maker we partner with, at least once a year.

Milestones

Our Journey So Far

Spring 2016

The First Workshop

Founded in a converted garage with three makers and a shared belief that local goods deserve a better platform. Our first collection featured hand-thrown ceramics and beeswax candles.

Autumn 2017

First Storefront Opens

Opened our brick-and-mortar space downtown, doubling as a retail shop and maker workspace. Partnered with 12 local artisans in the first month.

January 2019

Online Shop Launch

Brought our curated catalog online, making locally crafted goods accessible beyond our neighborhood. Shipped to all 50 states within the first quarter.

Summer 2021

100 Makers Strong

Reached our hundredth maker partnership — a milestone that reinforced our commitment to small-batch, ethically sourced production over mass-market convenience.

March 2024

Sustainability Certification

Achieved third-party sustainability certification across our entire supply chain. Every product now meets verified environmental and labor standards.

Community

More Than a Store

We host monthly maker markets, craft workshops, and community dinners. Our space is open to anyone who believes in the power of local, handmade goods.

Every quarter, we open our workshop for hands-on classes taught by the very artisans whose products line our shelves. From ceramics throwing to natural dye techniques, these sessions connect you directly with the craft behind the goods.

We also run a maker residency program, offering emerging artisans workspace, mentorship, and a platform to launch their first collections. Three of our current top sellers started as residents.
